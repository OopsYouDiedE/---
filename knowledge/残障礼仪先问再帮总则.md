## 一、「先问，再帮」的权威出处

### 1.1 United Spinal Association《Disability Etiquette》手册——"Ask Before You Help" 是第一条

[已多源确认——手册原文逐字核实]

- 手册 "The Basics" 第一条（p.4，逐字）："Just because someone has a disability, **don't assume she needs help**. If the setting is accessible, people with disabilities can usually get around fine. **Adults with disabilities want to be treated as independent people.** Offer assistance only if the person appears to need it. A person with a disability will oftentimes communicate when she needs help. **And if she does want help, ask how before you act.**"
  - 三层结构：①别假设需要帮助；②残障成年人希望被当独立的人对待；③对方要帮时，**先问怎么帮**再动手。
- Top 10 摘要版措辞："Ask before you help. If you offer and the person with a disability accepts, **follow their instructions. They know their capabilities.**"（对方接受了，就按对方的指示做——对方最清楚自己的能力。）

**来源**：
- United Spinal Association, *Disability Etiquette*: https://unitedspinal.org/pdf/DisabilityEtiquette.pdf 可信度：★★★★★（官方手册 PDF，逐页核实）
- Top 10 版: https://unitedspinal.org/top-10-disability-etiquette/ 可信度：★★★★☆

### 1.2 对方说不用，就是不用

[已多源确认]

- United Spinal p.6 "Don't Make Assumptions"（逐字）："People with disabilities are **the best judge of what they can or cannot do**. Don't make decisions for them about participating in any activity."（自己能做什么，残障者本人是最好的裁判。）
- Alliance for Equality of Blind Canadians："Offer assistance respectfully, and **don't be offended if they decline. Independence is part of the goal.**"（拒绝了别觉得被冒犯——独立本身就是目标。）
  - URL: https://www.blindcanadians.ca/post/how-to-interact-respectfully-with-a-blind-person-without-being-awkward 可信度：★★★★☆

### 1.3 对着本人说话

[已多源确认——手册原文逐字核实]

- United Spinal p.6 "Think Before You Speak"（逐字）："**Always speak directly to the person with a disability, not to his companion, aide or sign language interpreter.**"（永远直接对残障者本人说话，不对陪同者、助理或手语翻译说。）
- 适用于：坐轮椅者（别只对推轮椅的人说）、聋人（别只对翻译说，另见 knowledge/听障沟通与助听设备局限.md 第六节）、视障者、认知障碍者（纽约州政府页："Speak with them rather than about them"——跟他们说，不是当着面谈论他们）。

### 1.4 称呼原则（简要）

[已多源确认]

- United Spinal p.6 "Put the Person First"："Say 'person with a disability' rather than 'disabled person.'... If you are not sure what words to use, **ask**."（人在前，障碍在后；拿不准怎么称呼，问本人。）避免 "victim/sufferer" 一类剥夺能动性的词；说"使用轮椅的人"而非"被困在轮椅上的人"——原文："The wheelchair is... **liberating, not confining**."（轮椅是解放，不是禁锢。）
- 联合国《Disability-Inclusive Language Guidelines》（UN Geneva）：推荐 "persons with disabilities"；避免 "suffer from / confined to a wheelchair / wheelchair-bound / handicapped"；总原则：不确定就**问本人**如何自称。
  - URL: https://www.ungeneva.org/en/about/accessibility/disability-inclusive-language/annex-i 可信度：★★★★★
- **中文落点**：对应中文语境即"残疾人/残障人士"官方称谓、避免"残废"等贬义旧词；轮椅=工具不是牢笼。英文 person-first 构词差异不必搬进正文。

## 二、「强行帮助」为什么帮倒忙

### 2.1 物理层：抓，会把人抓倒

[已多源确认——手册原文逐字核实]

- United Spinal p.4 "Be Sensitive About Physical Contact"（逐字）："Some people with disabilities **depend on their arms for balance. Grabbing them, even if your intention is to assist, could knock them off balance.**"（很多残障者靠手臂维持平衡——你去抓，哪怕是想帮忙，可能直接把人抓倒。）
- United Spinal p.11（盲人节，逐字）："**Identify yourself before you make physical contact** with a person who is blind."；"People who are blind may need their arms for balance, so **offer your arm—don't take his**—if he needs to be guided."（先自报身份再碰；把你的手臂给他，不是抓他的。）
- United Spinal p.7（轮椅节，逐字）："**Don't push or touch a person's wheelchair; it's part of her personal space.** If you help someone down a curb without waiting for instructions, **you may dump her out of the chair.**"（不等指示就"帮"着下路缘，可能把人整个倒出轮椅。）
- United Spinal p.9（拐杖/手杖使用者，逐字）："People who use canes or crutches need their arms to balance themselves, **so never grab them**... **Always ask before helping.**"
- Chicago Lighthouse（作者本人视障）："**Never grab or pull a person who is trying to cross the street.** If you think we might need help, always ask; never assume."——这类"帮助""are not helpful at all... they are a distraction and **may even pose a danger**"（过马路时被抓＝分心＝危险）。
  - URL: https://chicagolighthouse.org/sandys-view/crossing-the-street/ 可信度：★★★★☆
- Blind Veterans UK："The most important thing is to **ask the person if they want assistance before you try to help**"；"Rather than grab their arm to guide them, ask if they'd like to hold your arm or shoulder"。
  - URL: https://www.blindveterans.org.uk/sight-loss-resources/how-to-guide-a-vision-impaired-person/ 可信度：★★★★☆
- AFB 医疗场景指南："DO ask if the individual needs assistance, **DO NOT assume**"；"**DO NOT grab or push the individual**"；"Explain what you are going to do before you do it"（动手前先说你要做什么）。
  - URL: https://www.afb.org/research-and-initiatives/research/toolkits/best-practices-guide-hospitals/serving-needs-healthcare-general-handout 可信度：★★★★★

### 2.2 心理层：未经请求的帮助＝暗示对方无能（同行评审研究）

[已多源确认——同行评审]

- Wang, Walker, Pietri & Ashburn-Nardo (2019), *Journal of Social Issues*：
  - "encounters with unsolicited help represent **one of the most common forms of ableism** experienced by individuals with visible physical or sensory disabilities."（未经请求的帮助，是可见身体/感官障碍者遭遇的最普遍的健全主义形式之一。）
  - 心理代价："can incur significant psychological costs because **it implies that the recipients lack the ability to take responsibility for their own welfare**."（它暗示接受者没有能力照顾自己。）
  - 两难困境："Although accepting unwanted help may **undermine their self-esteem and sense of autonomy**, refusing such help might have significant interpersonal repercussions"——收下伤自尊与自主感；拒绝又常被指责 "ungrateful, rude, and hyper-sensitive"（不识好歹）。实验发现盲人拒绝帮助后遭到的反弹比轮椅使用者更重。
  - URL: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7316393/ 可信度：★★★★★
- **章内落点**：这条研究解释了"先问"的分量——问一句，既避免物理危险，也把"你行不行"的评判权还给本人。

### 2.3 #JustAskDontGrab：被抓被拽是视障者的日常

[单一来源为主＋多机构定性一致]

- 英国视障活动家 Dr. Amy Kavanagh 因每日通勤中被陌生人抓、推、拽的经历发起 **#JustAskDontGrab** 运动，呼吁 "**ask for consent before offering assistance**"；获 Transport for All Awards 2020 年度运动奖。
  - URL: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amy_Kavanagh 可信度：★★★☆☆（维基条目，事件脉络可用，细节引语勿咬死）
- [口径说明] "抓着拽"作为盲人最常遇到的错误帮助：**没有统计调查支撑频率**，但 Chicago Lighthouse、Blind Veterans UK、AFB、United Spinal 各自独立反复举这个例子——写成"机构反复警示的典型场景"，不写成统计事实。

### 2.4 一条好用的细节：光喊"小心"没有信息

[单一来源——手册原文]

- United Spinal p.13："Hollering '**Look out!**' does not tell the person if he should stop, run, duck or jump."（光喊"小心！"，对方不知道该停、该跑、该低头还是该跳。）——警示要带内容："小心，右边台阶"。与视障引导"报路况要具体"同一逻辑。

## 三、写作口径备忘

- 「先问，再帮」三步完整链条：**先问（需要搭把手吗）→ 对方说不用就是不用 → 对方说要，就按对方说的做**。三步都有硬出处（1.1、1.2）。
- 本节是全章的"总开关"：后面每类人群的手法，第一步都回到这里。
- 物理危险（抓倒、倒出轮椅）与心理伤害（暗示无能）两条线并行，都有原文/研究支撑——正文可以一硬一软搭配写。
- 争议边界：Wang et al. 是美国样本的社会心理研究，结论谨慎表述为"研究发现"；不外推"中国残障者都这么想"。
